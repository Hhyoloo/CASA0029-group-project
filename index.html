04 Composite Habitat Evaluation Framework

From hotspots to Bird Habitat Potential.

Before the final explorer map, the project introduces a literature-informed composite index. It separates positive ecological support from negative urban pressure, so the attention zones are interpretable rather than decorative.

Bird Habitat Potential

Composite index used to classify attention zones

BHP = (w1Hi + w2Ci) - (w3Di + w4Ei) Positive ecological support minus negative urban pressure
HiHabitat Quality

Green space and water-related habitat support.

CiConnectivity

Potential corridor value and ecological continuity.

DiHuman Disturbance

Direct anthropogenic interaction such as roads or building density.

EiEnvironmental Pressure

Background ecological burdens such as heat, noise, light or pollution exposure.

Positive ecological support

Habitat quality and connectivity increase the potential of an area to support migratory bird activity.

  • Green cover and open space
  • Water proximity and blue corridors
  • Landscape continuity between hotspots

Negative ecological pressure

Disturbance and Environmental Pressure reduce suitability. This wording is more precise than the vague term stress.

  • Human Disturbance: direct urban interaction
  • Environmental Pressure: ambient environmental burden
  • Current proxy: building centroid density plus urban intensity

Why not call it Stress?

Environmental Pressure refers to background ecological burdens such as urban heat, noise exposure, light pollution or surrounding environmental stressors that may reduce habitat suitability.

05 Future

Towards bird-friendly cities.

Migratory birds are more than seasonal visitors. They are indicators of urban environmental quality and ecological resilience.

Planning the flyway

Protecting bird habitats is not only about conserving species, but about maintaining the ecological resilience of the city itself.

Selected strategy

Protect stable habitat cores

Start from repeated hotspots that overlap measurable habitat. These are the strongest candidates for conservation attention.

1

Find

Identify repeated seasonal hotspots.

2

Overlay

Compare green, water and building density.

3

Classify

Separate cores, corridors and pressure zones.

4

Plan

Use evidence to guide bird-friendly urban design.

Limitations

Records are influenced by observer behaviour and accessibility.

Data caution

Building values are centroid density, not land-cover area.

Team use
